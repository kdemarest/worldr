## **Ashanti Proverbs**

It is the fool's sheep that break loose twice.  
By the time the fool has learned the game, the players have left.  
A crab does not give birth to a bird.  
When the cock is drunk, he forgets about the hawk.  
Fire and gunpowder do not sleep together.  
X Hunger is felt by a slave and hunger is felt by a king.  
One cannot both feast and become rich.  
The moon moves slowly, but it crosses the town.  
If you are in hiding, don't light a fire.  
Money is sharper than a sword.  
No one tests the depth of a river with both feet.  
It is when you climb a good tree that we lift you.  
One head does not hold council.  
A child breaks a snail, not a tortoise.  
The wise are spoken to in proverbs, not plain language.  
It is to watch both ways that two antelopes walk together.  
If an animal will bite you, it will be from your cloth.  
If a bird does not fly, it goes to bed hungry.  
Nobody knows the beginning of a great man.  
X When one father dies, another father lives.  
Disgrace does not befit the child of an Akan.  
Nobody teaches a child God.  
If the party is to be entertaining, it starts in the morning.  
If a naked man promises you a cloth, listen to his name.  
Someone’s delicacy is the intestines of an odompo.  
We send a wise person, not one with long legs.  
For the animal who does not have a tail, it is God who sweeps his body.  
It is the sweetest papaya that has the longest stick under it.  
When you remove one broomstick it breaks but, not when you put them together.  
All fingers are not the same.  
If someone does not know, another teaches.  
When a child learns how to wash his hands, he eats with adults.  
X If you are born atop a mound, it does not take you long to grow tall.  
The back of your hand is not as soft as your palm.  
If it happens to your neighbor, it has happened to a tree.  
If someone kills by mistake, we do not butcher by mistake.  
If you see your neighbor’s hut burning, fetch water for yours.  
Wise knots are loosened by wise men.
