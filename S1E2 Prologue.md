# Scenario: Prologue

**Team Objective:** When a plot is overheard at a cricket match, the team must save an Sicilian abbot from death or kidnapping.

All is not as it appears. The abbot is Prince Regent George IV, and the danger is a Black Relic buried below the Cosmati pavement before the tomb of Saint King Edward the Confessor at Westminster Abbey.

## Player Motivations

* <character> gets paid to "watch the front of the alley" by Streaky, a younger kid who he hates. Streaky, Duff and Old Farm. But, being down on his luck, shillings are shillings.
* Archaeologist, with a pocket full of grant money, is there with his helper Franklin, and a device. Oliver is meant to watch the equipment and take position to help with measurements, The objective is to use the Oscillatory Radio Vibraphone and vibrations from the trampling herd of people to attempt to identify ruins or major objects in Lord's Cricket Grounds. Also, he can't help but be fascinated by the anthropological significance of such a gathering and its ramifications. The grant money will be used to hire urchins to hold silver sticks to help with triangulation.
* Con is there to stay sharp, identifying unusual characters, tracking them, and discovering their motives. It is a simple exercise to keep in top form.

* The Oscillator is a large pan marked with a grid of lines, holding a very thin sheet of 100% alcohol. It must be strung to the walls with twine, leveled, and as it vibrates intersecting wave peaks must be noticed. These can be extrapolated by angle to the ground above as a 3D point cloud indicating shapes. To image the area , you must take above ground readings, then enter the sewers, set up the equipment, which takes about an hour, and take measurements.

## Backstory

George IV feigns a dandy, actually battling the Corruptor in concert with the Benedictines. He visits the tomb of Edward the Confessor sometimes because the tomb holds words of power that must be re-learned every month to ultra-clarify the mind, and he uses this to plan and see forward in his work against the Corruptor. But our story starts at Lord's Cricket Grounds at the 11th annual Gentlemen vs Players match.

## Setup

While attending (or participating in) a grand cricket match, enmity between Lord Frederick Beauclerk and William Lambert erupts. Their intrigues and jealousies explode into a match-fixing scandal with the top player William Lambert being banned from playing at Lord's Cricket Ground for life.

* **Beauclerk** - Vicar of Kimpton and a deady dull preacher. At age 44 still puts cricket first. Captain of the All-England team. Holds world record for highest individual innings.
* **Lambert** - a pro cricketer and the first man ever to score two centuries.

The setting is Lord's Cricket Ground. It is the 11th annual Gentlemen vs Players match, when the upper crust go against the professionals. The Gentlemen, of course, are lead by the world record holder Lord Frederick Beauclerk. The players, by William Lambert, the first man to bat two centuries.

It is a grudge match and the crowd is huge. Everyone knows that only, seven years ago, Lambert bowled wide against Beauclerk, who lost his temper and his wicket. Lambert won by 15. Beauclerk was humiliated. The day is bright and the crowd is huge, perhaps a thousand people milling about, setting up lawn chairs. Some sit in pavilions erected before dawn, as servants serve them hors d'ouvres. The lower classes sit on blankets. Bookies roam, crying their odds and kids act as "runners" exchanging bets and tickets.

## Cosmati Research

Westminster has two Cosmati pavements:

1. In front of the the altar  
2. Around the tomb of Edward the Confessor

The PCs may learn about #1 by consulting eg a scholar at the library or certain Westminster monks. The researcher they might find is **Reginald Jones**. You learn where he breakfasts. He can reveal, as can certain monks at Westminster, the following:

* Odoricus was the monk that handled architecture for parts of Westminster
* The tile before the altar is a Cosmati design, utterly unique. "The extraordinary pavement in front of the high altar at Westminster Abbey was installed starting in 1268. The fascinating design has an overall fourfold symmetry and certain elements repeat, but on the other hand every part of the design is unique. The men who did the work came from Rome. This sort of abstract tiling was a Roman specialty, called in the west of Europe "Cosmati" after one of the leading families in the business. The supervisor of this project was named Odoricus, about whom hardly anything is known"
* If they ask at Westminster, they may learn that a visiting Italian abbot is to be given special accord, and full access to any part of the abbey. This is very rare.
